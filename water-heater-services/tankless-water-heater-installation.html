Why Choose Tankless Water Heaters?

Frequently Asked Questions

The installation of a tankless water heater typically takes 4 to 6 hours, depending on the complexity of the setup and any additional work needed for venting or gas piping.

While it is possible to install a tankless water heater yourself, it is usually recommended to hire a professional. Improper installation can lead to issues with ventilation, gas connections, and efficiency.

The size of the tankless water heater you need depends on your household's hot water demand and climate. It's best to consult with a professional to assess your specific needs and ensure optimal performance.

Proper venting is crucial for the safe operation of gas-powered tankless water heaters. They require stainless steel or PVC vent pipes that are corrosion-resistant and capable of handling high temperatures. Consult the manufacturer’s guidelines for specific requirements.

In many cases, upgrading your home’s gas line is necessary when installing a tankless water heater because they often require a higher flow rate than traditional tank water heaters. A professional installer can evaluate your current gas system and recommend any needed upgrades.
