Frequently Asked Questions

A slow-draining sink can often be fixed by clearing any debris or hair from the drain. Start by removing the sink stopper and cleaning it. You can also use a plunger or a drain snake to clear any blockages in the pipes. Avoid using chemical drain cleaners as they may damage your pipes.

To prevent pipes from freezing, ensure they are insulated, especially in colder climates. Keep the bathroom warm, allow faucets to drip slightly during very cold weather, and open cabinet doors to allow warm air to circulate around the plumbing.

A running toilet is usually due to a faulty flapper, float, or fill valve. Check the flapper to ensure it seals properly and replace it if necessary. Adjust the float to ensure it shuts off the water at the correct level, or replace the fill valve if it's malfunctioning.

To unclog a shower drain, first remove any visible hair or debris. You can use a plunger or a plumbing snake to clear further blockages. For a natural solution, try pouring a mixture of baking soda and vinegar down the drain, followed by hot water.

Low water pressure can be caused by clogged pipes, mineral buildup, or issues with the water supply line. Clean any aerators or showerheads to remove mineral deposits. Check for any leaks in your plumbing system and ensure the main water valve is fully open.
