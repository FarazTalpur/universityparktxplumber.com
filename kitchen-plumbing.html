Frequently Asked Questions

The most common cause of a leaky kitchen faucet is worn out washers or gaskets. Over time, these components can wear down, leading to drips and leaks.

To unclog a kitchen sink, you can try using a plunger or a mixture of baking soda and vinegar followed by hot water. If these methods don't work, a plumber's snake can be used to remove more stubborn clogs.

A slow-draining kitchen sink is often due to a partial blockage in the drain. This can be caused by a build-up of grease, food particles, or soap scum. Regular cleaning and using strainers can help prevent this issue.

To prevent odors from your kitchen sink, regularly clean the drain with a mixture of baking soda and vinegar, and use lemon peels or a few drops of essential oil for a fresh scent. Ensuring that food waste is properly disposed of can also help.

If your garbage disposal is jammed, first turn it off to avoid damage. Use a hex key to manually turn the disposal's motor, or use tongs to remove any visible obstructions. Reset the unit by pressing the reset button located on the underside of the disposal unit.
