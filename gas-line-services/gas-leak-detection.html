Frequently Asked Questions

Common signs of a gas leak include a rotten egg or sulfur smell, a hissing or whistling sound near a gas line, dead or dying vegetation in an otherwise moist area, and physical symptoms such as dizziness or nausea.

If you suspect a gas leak, immediately evacuate the area and avoid using any electronics or electrical devices. Once you are at a safe distance, call your gas company or emergency services to report the leak.

To prevent gas leaks, ensure regular maintenance and inspection of gas appliances and pipelines, install carbon monoxide detectors, and adhere to the manufacturer's instructions when using gas-powered devices.

A gas detector is recommended as it can provide early warnings of a gas leak. It works by monitoring the concentration of gas in the air and sounding an alarm when it exceeds a safe threshold.

Gas appliances should be inspected at least once a year by a qualified professional to ensure they are operating safely and efficiently.
